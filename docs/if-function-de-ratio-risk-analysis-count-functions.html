Exercise 4 IF Function: D/E Ratio Risk Analysis + COUNT Functions

Learning Outcomes

  • Analyse company debt-equity data for risk assessment
  • Apply logical formulas (IF, Nested IF) for risk classification
  • Use statistical functions (COUNT, COUNTA, COUNTIF, COUNTIFS)
  • Create charts for leverage comparison

4.1 Dataset

Company Debt (Rs) Equity (Rs) D/E Ratio Risk Level Leverage Category Interest Coverage Investment Recommendation
Company A 500 1000 0.5 Low Under-Leveraged 4.00 BUY
Company B 1200 800 1.5 High Over-Leveraged 1.33 CAUTION
Company C 300 1500 0.2 Low Under-Leveraged 10.00 BUY
Company D 700 1000 0.7 Medium Under-Leveraged 2.86 BUY

D/E Risk Classification:
- < 0.5 → Low
- 0.5 – 1.0 → Medium
- 1.0 – 1.5 → High
- > 1.5 → Very High

Download Debt-Equity Data Excel

4.3 Data Entry & Formulas

4.3.1 Structure in Excel

Column Description
A Company
B–C Inputs (Debt, Equity)
D D/E Ratio
E Risk Level
F Leverage Category
G Interest Coverage
H Investment Recommendation

4.3.2 Formulas Used

Calculation Formula
D/E Ratio =B3/C3
Risk Level =IF(D3<0.5,“Low”,IF(D3<=1,“Medium”,IF(D3<=1.5,“High”,“Very High”)))
Leverage Category =IF(D3>1,“Over-Leveraged”,“Under-Leveraged”)

4.4 COUNT Functions Analysis

Calculation Formula
Count numeric D/E values =COUNT(D3:D6)
Count non-blank companies =COUNTA(A3:A6)
Companies with D/E > 1 =COUNTIF(D3:D6,“>1”)
High Risk companies =COUNTIF(E3:E6,“High”)
Empty cells =COUNTBLANK(D3:D6)

4.7 Interpretation

Companies A, C, and D maintain healthy financial structures with lower debt levels, making them suitable for investment. Company B shows higher financial risk due to excessive leverage and lower interest coverage.

4.8 Sorting

Example
